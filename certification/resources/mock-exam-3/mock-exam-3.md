# DP-700 Mock Exam 3

> [!warning] This is the hardest of the three mocks — take it last
> Mock 3 is deliberately built to over-index on documented traps, edge cases, and cross-domain scenarios. It's calibrated for someone who has already **cleared Mock 1 and Mock 2** (35+/50 on each). Attempting it cold, before the first two mocks, will misrepresent your actual readiness — the difficulty mix here (36% Hard) is not representative of the real exam's typical spread on its own; it's a stress test for the corners Mocks 1–2 already confirmed you know.

## Instructions

- **Time limit**: ==100 minutes== (50 questions including a 5-question case study — mirrors the real DP-700's own duration exactly)
- **Questions**: 50 questions total — 45 standalone multiple-choice (A/B/C/D) + 1 case-study block (Qs 46–50) with 5 linked sub-questions
- **Aids**: No notes, no documentation, no topic files open in another tab — attempt from memory first
- **Scoring**: Check answers only after completing all 50 questions
- **Review**: For any incorrect answers, use the [debrief](./mock-exam-3-debrief.md) to find the topic file to re-read

> [!tip] Take it timed in your browser
> Mock 3 is also available as a bank in the **live practice quiz** (`practice/index.html`) — pick the timed exam mode and the **DP-700 — Mock Exam 3** bank. Progress saves to your browser only.

> [!warning] Treat this as a real exam attempt
> Resist the urge to look up answers mid-session. The case-study block at the end mirrors the real DP-700 format — read the whole scenario once before answering Q46–Q50, since you can't return to the scenario after submitting.

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Correct Answers | Result |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| 1000  | 50 / 50        | Perfect |
| 800+  | 40+ / 50       | Strong pass |
| 700   | ~35 / 50       | Passing (minimum) |
| 600   | ~30 / 50       | Near miss — targeted re-study via the debrief map |
| <600  | <30 / 50       | More study needed |

**Passing score**: 700 out of 1000. The 35/50 rule-of-thumb assumes an even, linear mapping across all 50 questions — the real exam's scoring model weights questions and applies psychometric adjustments Microsoft doesn't publish, so treat ~35/50 as a directional signal, not a guarantee. A 33–34/50 result on this mock isn't necessarily a fail on the real thing, and a 36/50 isn't necessarily a pass — use the debrief's domain breakdown, not just the raw score, to judge readiness.

> [!note] Mock 3 is harder by design — recalibrate your expectations
> Because this mock over-indexes on Hard questions (36%, vs. 26% on Mocks 1–2), a slightly lower raw score here than you got on Mocks 1–2 doesn't automatically mean you regressed. Weigh **which** questions you missed (see the debrief's sub-band guidance) more heavily than the raw number.
